1. TITLE

A Phase III, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled, Multicenter
Study to Evaluate the Efficacy and Safety of an Investigational
Anti-PCSK9 Monoclonal Antibody in Statin-Intolerant Patients with
Heterozygous Familial Hypercholesterolaemia


2. SYNOPSIS

Study Phase:        Phase III
Design:             Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 1:1
Primary Endpoint:   % change from baseline in LDL-C at Week 24
Target Enrollment:  360 patients
Treatment:          Subcutaneous injection every 4 weeks (at clinic)
Duration:           52 weeks treatment + 24 weeks follow-up


3. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE

Heterozygous familial hypercholesterolaemia (HeFH) is an autosomal dominant
disorder causing lifelong LDL-C elevation and markedly increased cardiovascular
risk. Statins are first-line treatment but intolerance affects 5-10% of patients
on high-intensity therapy.

PCSK9 inhibition has emerged as the most effective lipid-lowering strategy for
statin-intolerant patients. Approved PCSK9 inhibitors require fortnightly dosing.
The investigational product evaluated in this study has an extended half-life
(~17 days) enabling monthly administration, potentially improving adherence.

Phase I data in healthy volunteers established safety, tolerability, and
pharmacokinetic profile. Monthly dosing achieved exposures consistent with
robust PCSK9 inhibition per PK/PD modelling.


4. STUDY OBJECTIVES

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE
To evaluate the effect of the investigational product versus placebo on LDL-C
as measured by percent change from baseline at Week 24.

SECONDARY OBJECTIVES
  1. % change from baseline in ApoB at Week 24
  2. % change from baseline in Lp(a) at Week 24
  3. % change from baseline in non-HDL-C at Week 24
  4. Proportion achieving LDL-C <1.8 mmol/L at Week 24
  5. Proportion achieving LDL-C <1.4 mmol/L at Week 24
  6. MACE through Week 52 (adjudicated)
  7. Safety and tolerability


5. PATIENT ELIGIBILITY

INCLUSION CRITERIA
  1. Age >= 18 years
  2. Confirmed HeFH (Simon Broome or Dutch Lipid Clinic criteria, score >=6)
  3. LDL-C >= 3.5 mmol/L on stable non-statin therapy for >= 8 weeks
  4. Documented statin intolerance:
       a. CK elevation >= 4x ULN on >= 2 statins, OR
       b. Myalgia with symptom recurrence on rechallenge with >= 2 statins
  5. No PCSK9 inhibitor therapy in the prior 12 months
  6. Fasting triglycerides < 4.5 mmol/L

EXCLUSION CRITERIA
  1. Homozygous FH
  2. Active liver disease or ALT/AST > 3x ULN
  3. eGFR < 30 mL/min
  4. MI, stroke, or coronary revascularisation within 3 months
  5. Known hypersensitivity to monoclonal antibody therapy
  6. Current participation in another interventional trial
  7. Pregnancy or breastfeeding


6. INVESTIGATIONAL PRODUCT

Fully human IgG1 anti-PCSK9 monoclonal antibody, 150 mg/mL, SC injection.
Dose: 150 mg SC every 4 weeks.
Route: Subcutaneous, administered by study nurse at clinic.
Supply: Pre-filled single-use auto-injector, 1 mL.
Placebo: Matching auto-injector containing vehicle only.

8. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS

360 patients (180 per arm) provides 95% power to detect a 50 percentage point
difference in LDL-C change at two-sided alpha 0.05.

Primary: MMRM with % change from baseline in LDL-C; fixed effects: treatment,
visit, treatment-by-visit, baseline LDL-C, stratification factors.

Missing data: Multiple imputation under MAR; tipping-point sensitivity.


9. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

Conducted in accordance with Declaration of Helsinki (2013), ICH E6(R2) GCP,
and applicable national regulations. Ethics Committee approval required before
enrollment. All patients provide written informed consent.
