SECTION 1 -- PROTOCOL SYNOPSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Study Phase             : Phase III
Design                  : Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, 1:1
Primary Endpoint        : % change from baseline in LDL-C at Week 24
Secondary Endpoints     : ApoB, non-HDL-C, Lp(a), MACE (Week 52), safety

Treatment Duration      : 52 weeks
Follow-up               : 24 weeks post-treatment
Countries               : Spain, Germany, Netherlands, Poland
IMP Administration      : 150 mg SC injection every 4 weeks (at clinic)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2 -- BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Familial hypercholesterolaemia (FH) is an autosomal dominant disorder caused
by loss-of-function mutations in the LDL receptor (LDLR), leading to lifelong
elevation of LDL cholesterol and markedly increased cardiovascular risk.
Heterozygous FH (HeFH) affects approximately 1 in 250 individuals worldwide.

Statins are first-line treatment for HeFH. However, statin intolerance affects
5-10% of patients on high-intensity statin therapy. Current alternatives
(ezetimibe, bile acid sequestrants) produce modest LDL-C reductions of 15-25%.

PCSK9 inhibitors reduce LDL-C by 50-60% but require fortnightly dosing.
BRC-883 (Cardanezumab) is a novel fully human IgG1 anti-PCSK9 monoclonal
antibody with an extended half-life (~17 days) enabling monthly (q4w) dosing,
potentially improving adherence while maintaining comparable efficacy.

Phase II data (BIORCE-CAR-2022-005, n=148, 24 weeks):
  LDL-C reduction: -57.3% (BRC-883) vs -2.8% (placebo), p<0.0001
  ApoB reduction: -46.9%, p<0.0001
  Lp(a) reduction: -23.8%, p=0.002
  Injection site reactions: 8.1% (mild, none requiring discontinuation)
  No MACE, no neutralizing antibodies

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 3 -- STUDY OBJECTIVES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY OBJECTIVE
  To evaluate effect of BRC-883 vs. placebo on LDL-C (% change from baseline
  at Week 24).

SECONDARY OBJECTIVES
  1. % change from baseline in ApoB at Week 24
  2. % change from baseline in Lp(a) at Week 24
  3. % change from baseline in non-HDL-C at Week 24
  4. Proportion achieving LDL-C <1.8 mmol/L at Week 24
  5. Proportion achieving LDL-C <1.4 mmol/L at Week 24
  6. MACE through Week 52 (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke)
  7. Safety and tolerability

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4 -- PATIENT ELIGIBILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INCLUSION CRITERIA
  1. Age >=18 years
  2. Confirmed HeFH (Simon Broome or Dutch Lipid Clinic criteria, score >=6)
  3. LDL-C >=3.5 mmol/L on stable non-statin therapy >=8 weeks, or no therapy
  4. Documented statin intolerance:
       a. CK elevation >=4x ULN on >=2 statins, OR
       b. Myalgia confirmed on rechallenge with >=2 statins
  5. No PCSK9 inhibitor in prior 12 months
  6. Fasting TG <4.5 mmol/L

EXCLUSION CRITERIA
  1. Homozygous FH
  2. Active liver disease or ALT/AST >3x ULN
  3. eGFR <30 mL/min
  4. MI, stroke, or coronary revascularisation within 3 months
  5. Known hypersensitivity to monoclonal antibody therapy
  6. Concurrent interventional trial
  7. Pregnancy or breastfeeding

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5 -- STATISTICAL ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sample size: 360 patients (180/arm) provides 95% power to detect -52% LDL-C
reduction vs -2% placebo, two-sided alpha 0.05 (10% dropout assumed).

Primary analysis: MMRM -- % change from baseline in LDL-C; fixed effects:
treatment, visit, treatment-by-visit, baseline LDL-C, country.
