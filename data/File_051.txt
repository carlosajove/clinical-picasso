4. EFFICACY RESULTS

4.1  Patient Disposition and Analysis Populations

Of 480 patients randomised, 477 received at least one dose of study treatment
(3 patients withdrew consent before first dose). The mITT population comprised
477 patients (320 velagostat, 157 placebo). The PP population comprised 431
patients; 46 excluded due to protocol deviations, most commonly missed cognitive
assessments outside the protocol window (n=31).

  Disposition              Velagostat (n=320)   Placebo (n=160)
  -------------------------------------------------------------------
  Completed 96 weeks         241 (75.3%)          119 (74.4%)
  Discontinued                79 (24.7%)           41 (25.6%)
    AE                         22  (6.9%)           11  (6.9%)
    Withdrawal                 28  (8.8%)           16 (10.0%)
    Lost to follow-up          14  (4.4%)            8  (5.0%)
    Death                      15  (4.7%)            6  (3.8%)

  Deaths on study: 21 total. All reviewed by independent adjudication
  committee. No deaths considered treatment-related.

4.3  Key Secondary Endpoints at Week 78

  ADAS-Cog13:    +2.4 vs +4.1  -- difference -1.7 points (p=0.003)
  ADCS-ADL-MCI:  -3.2 vs -5.8  -- difference +2.6 points (p=0.001)
  MMSE:          -1.8 vs -3.1  -- difference +1.3 points (p=0.012)
  Amyloid PET:   -2.9 vs -1.6  -- NS (p=0.44) -- confirms non-amyloid MOA
  Tau PET SUVR (entorhinal): -26.4% vs -3.8% (p<0.001)
  Tau PET SUVR (temporal):   -23.1% vs -3.2% (p<0.001)

  All key secondary endpoints met statistical significance.

4.4  Exploratory Biomarkers

  Plasma p-tau 181 (Week 78): -38.4% (velagostat) vs -2.1% (placebo), p<0.001
  Plasma NfL (Week 78):       +4.2% vs +18.7% (p=0.002) -- attenuation of
                               neurodegeneration marker increase
  APOE e4 subgroup (n=284):   LS mean diff -0.81 (p<0.001)
  Non-e4 subgroup (n=193):    LS mean diff -0.59 (p=0.018)
  No significant treatment-by-genotype interaction (p=0.31).

  CSF sub-study (n=60, paired baseline/Week 78):
    p-tau 181: -41.2% vs -3.4% (p<0.001)
    Total tau:  -28.7% vs -1.8% (p<0.001)
    Strongest evidence of central tau target engagement.

4.5  Time to Confirmed Cognitive Decline

  Pre-specified exploratory endpoint (CDR-SB increase >=1 point, confirmed):
    Velagostat: median 62.4 weeks (95% CI 54.1-NR)
    Placebo:    median 44.8 weeks (95% CI 38.2-54.6)
    HR 0.64 (95% CI 0.49-0.84), p=0.001 -- 36% risk reduction
