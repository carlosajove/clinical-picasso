## What Has Changed?

### More Frequent Liver Blood Tests

During the ongoing study, 3 patients (out of 189 treated so far) had an
increase in their liver enzymes (a blood marker called ALT) to more than
5 times the upper limit of normal. In all 3 cases the levels returned to
normal after the study medicine was paused — no patient suffered lasting
liver damage. However, we want to monitor this more carefully.

**What this means for you:** You will now have liver blood tests at
**Weeks 4, 8, 12, and 24** (in addition to your regular blood tests).
This requires no extra visits — the extra tests will be done alongside
your existing blood draw at these visits.

**If your ALT rises above 5x normal:** The study medicine will be paused
and your study doctor will contact you within 48 hours. In most cases
the medicine can be safely restarted once levels return to normal.
