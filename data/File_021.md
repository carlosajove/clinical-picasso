# CSR-003 — CLINICAL STUDY REPORT
## Study: BIORCE-NEU-2020-002
## Phase II Study of BRC-209 (Velagostat) in Early Alzheimer's Disease
### FINAL REPORT | Date: 30 June 2023 | CONFIDENTIAL

**Data Cut-off:** 31 March 2023
**Total patients enrolled:** 94 (BRC-209: n=63; Placebo: n=31)
**Sponsor:** Biorce Therapeutics S.L.

## SYNOPSIS

**Primary Endpoint:** Change from baseline in CDR-SB at Week 52

**Result:** BRC-209 400 mg QD produced a statistically significant reduction
in CDR-SB progression vs. placebo at 52 weeks (LS mean difference: -0.54
points; 95% CI -1.04 to -0.04; p=0.035).

**Key Secondary Findings:**
- Tau PET SUVR (entorhinal): -31% BRC-209 vs. -4% placebo (p=0.003)
- Tau PET SUVR (temporal): -28% vs. -4% (p<0.001)
- Plasma p-tau 181: -34% vs. -1% (p=0.001)
- MMSE: No significant difference (p=0.21)
- ADAS-Cog 13: Numerical improvement (p=0.09, non-significant)

## 4. BIOMARKERS

Tau PET findings represent the most robust signal of target engagement.
Reduction of -31% entorhinal and -28% temporal is considered meaningful.

Amyloid PET: No significant change in either arm (p=0.44), confirming
BRC-209 does not act through amyloid clearance.

## 6. CONCLUSIONS

BRC-209 400 mg QD demonstrated statistically significant slowing of CDR-SB
progression at 52 weeks and robust tau biomarker target engagement. The
Phase III study (BIORCE-NEU-2022-004) is powered to detect a 0.7-point
difference at Week 78, which would be clinically meaningful.
