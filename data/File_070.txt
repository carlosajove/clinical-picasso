5. SAFETY RESULTS

5.1 Overview of Adverse Events

A total of 148 patients received at least one dose of study treatment
(74 active, 74 placebo). The median duration of exposure was 23.8 weeks
(range 1.1-24.6) for the active arm and 23.4 weeks (range 0.9-24.4)
for the placebo arm.

  Safety parameter             Active (n=74)   Placebo (n=74)
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Any TEAE                      68.9%           67.6%
  Grade >=3 TEAE                 5.4%            6.8%
  SAE                            2.7%            3.9%
  Discontinuation due to AE      1.4%            1.4%
  Deaths                         0               0

No deaths occurred during the study. The two SAEs in the active arm were
a hospitalisation for cellulitis (unrelated) and an ALT elevation (possibly
related; resolved). The four SAEs in the placebo arm comprised two
cardiovascular events and two infections, all unrelated to study treatment.

5.2 Common Treatment-Emergent Adverse Events

TEAEs occurring in >= 3% of patients in either arm:

  TEAE (MedDRA PT)          Active n(%)   Placebo n(%)
  ------------------------------------------------------
  Injection site reaction     6 (8.1%)     3 (3.9%)
  Nasopharyngitis             5 (6.8%)     5 (7.1%)
  Back pain                   4 (5.4%)     5 (6.5%)
  Headache                    3 (4.1%)     4 (5.2%)
  Myalgia                     2 (3.4%)     3 (4.5%)
  Influenza                   2 (2.7%)     3 (4.1%)
  ALT increased               1 (1.4%)     0

All injection site reactions were Grade 1-2. Mean duration 1.8 days
(range 1-4 days). No anaphylactic reactions. No discontinuations.

Myalgia was not elevated in the active arm vs placebo (3.4% vs 4.5%),
an important finding given the statin-intolerant study population.

5.3 Cardiovascular Events

All potential cardiovascular events were adjudicated by an independent CEC.
During the 24-week treatment period:

  MACE (adjudicated): 0 events (active) vs 2 events (placebo)

The two MACE events in the placebo arm were attributable to underlying
high-risk cardiovascular disease. The study was not powered for MACE.

5.4 Hepatic Safety

One patient in the active arm experienced ALT elevation to 3.2x ULN at
Week 8. Asymptomatic, detected on routine monitoring. Drug held 3 weeks;
ALT returned to 1.1x ULN. Patient rechallenged without recurrence. Assessed
as possibly related to study drug.

No bilirubin elevation. No Hy's Law criteria met.

Mean ALT change Week 24: +3.2 U/L (active) vs +1.8 U/L (placebo), p=0.41.

Given the emerging hepatic signal in the ongoing Phase III study (3 additional
ALT >5x ULN cases), enhanced LFT monitoring has been added to the Phase III
protocol as an amendment.

5.5 Immunogenicity

ADA incidence: 4.1% (3/74). No neutralising antibodies. No relationship
between ADA status and PK, efficacy, or adverse events.

5.6 Neurocognitive Safety

CANTAB battery at baseline and Week 24 (exploratory). No statistically
significant or clinically meaningful differences on any domain (sustained
attention, spatial working memory, pattern recognition memory). Consistent
with published data for approved PCSK9 inhibitors.

5.7 Conclusions -- Safety

The investigational product at 150 mg SC q4w demonstrated a favourable
safety profile over 24 weeks. No deaths. SAE rate lower in active arm.
Injection site reactions mild and self-limiting. Single hepatic enzyme
elevation resolved on drug hold. No myalgia excess. No neurocognitive
safety signal. No immunogenicity impact.

The safety profile supports continued development at 150 mg SC q4w.
Enhanced hepatic monitoring implemented in the Phase III programme.
