SECTION 5: WHAT ARE THE POSSIBLE RISKS?

The study medicine is experimental. Not all risks are known. Based on studies
carried out so far, the following side effects have been observed. Report any
new or worsening symptom to the study team immediately.

VERY COMMON (affecting more than 1 in 10 people):

  Diarrhoea
    Most cases are mild and can be managed with diet changes and loperamide.
    Severe diarrhoea (more than 6 episodes per day) should be reported the
    same day.

  Skin rash
    A spot-like rash, similar to acne, commonly on face, neck, chest, back.
    Most cases mild to moderate. Topical or oral antibiotics can help.
    Usually appears in the first 2-3 weeks then stabilises.

  Nausea (feeling sick)
    Usually mild and most common in the first week of each treatment cycle.
    You will be given anti-nausea tablets (ondansetron) before your morning
    dose during the first week of each new cycle.

  Tiredness / Fatigue
    Commonly reported. Usually mild to moderate. Let the study team know if
    fatigue is affecting your daily activities.

COMMON (affecting 1 in 10 to 1 in 100 people):

  Sore skin around nails (paronychia)
    Warm water soaks and topical antiseptics usually sufficient.
    Inform the study team as early as possible.

  Mouth sores (stomatitis)
    Usually mild. Rinse with saltwater or sodium bicarbonate solution.

  Increased liver enzymes (on blood tests only)
    Usually resolves without treatment change. Monitored by regular blood tests.

UNCOMMON BUT SERIOUS (fewer than 1 in 100 people):

  Lung inflammation (pneumonitis / interstitial lung disease)
    Approximately 1 in 25 patients in previous studies developed lung
    inflammation. Symptoms: new or worsening shortness of breath, dry cough,
    or fever. If you experience these: STOP the study medicine and contact
    the study team or attend the nearest emergency department immediately.
    Do not wait for your next scheduled visit.

RISKS OF STUDY PROCEDURES:
  Blood tests: Bruising or minor discomfort at needle site.
  CT scans: Low level radiation. Benefit of cancer monitoring outweighs risk.
  Tumour biopsy (if required): Discomfort, bleeding, infection risk.

UNKNOWN RISKS:
  This medicine is still being tested. There may be side effects not yet seen.
  You will be monitored closely throughout.


SECTION 6: WHAT ARE THE POSSIBLE BENEFITS?

You may benefit directly if the medicine shrinks your cancer or slows its
growth. Previous studies showed approximately 1 in 3 patients with your type
of cancer responded to this treatment. We cannot guarantee you will respond.

Even if the medicine does not help you, your participation will help doctors
understand this type of cancer better and may help future patients.


SECTION 7: WHAT OTHER TREATMENTS ARE AVAILABLE?

If you decide not to take part, your doctor will discuss other options.
These may include chemotherapy, immunotherapy, or other clinical trials.
Not joining will not limit your access to standard care.


SECTION 8: WHAT HAPPENS IF NEW INFORMATION BECOMES AVAILABLE?

If new information emerges that might affect your willingness to continue,
your doctor will tell you as soon as possible. You may be asked to sign an
updated version of this consent form. You are free to withdraw at any time.


SECTION 9: YOUR PERSONAL DATA

Your data will be handled in strict accordance with EU GDPR and applicable
national law. Study records use a number code, not your name. Authorised
representatives of the sponsor, Ethics Committee, and regulatory authorities
may inspect your records while maintaining confidentiality.

You have the right to access, correct, or restrict processing of your data.


SECTION 10: COSTS AND PAYMENTS

No cost to you. Study medicine provided free. Reasonable travel costs
reimbursed. There is no payment for participation.
