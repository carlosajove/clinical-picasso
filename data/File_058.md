## 3. DSMB Deliberations (Sponsor not present)

The DSMB deliberated independently for approximately 40 minutes.

**ILD/Pneumonitis:** Prof. Lindqvist noted the 6% incidence is higher than
osimertinib (~2%) and the ~3% in IB Edition 1. Dr. Taniguchi reviewed imaging
descriptions: all 4 consistent with drug-induced pneumonitis (bilateral peripheral
GGO). All responded appropriately to drug hold and corticosteroids. Class-effect
mechanism, manageable. Stopping rule not triggered.

**Nausea:** Dr. Webb noted nausea concentrated in Cycle 1. Prophylactic
antiemetics (ondansetron) recommended for Days 1-7 of each cycle.

**Overall Benefit-Risk:** Unblinded efficacy data reviewed (ORR 29%, median PFS
5.4 months). DSMB unanimously agreed benefit-risk remains favourable. Study
should continue.

---

## 4. DSMB Recommendations

**RECOMMENDATION 1 — STUDY CONTINUATION**
Study may continue enrolment without modification to stopping rules.

**RECOMMENDATION 2 — PROTOCOL AMENDMENT (URGENT, within 30 days)**
- Mandatory baseline HRCT for all newly enrolled patients
- Enhanced on-treatment HRCT monitoring
- Prophylactic ondansetron 8mg PO Days 1-7 of each new cycle
- Exclude patients with prior radiation to >20% of lung volume

**RECOMMENDATION 3 — IB UPDATE**
IB must be updated to reflect higher-than-anticipated ILD incidence.

**RECOMMENDATION 4 — NEXT REVIEW**
Next DSMB review triggered at patient #90 (not #100) given ILD signal.
