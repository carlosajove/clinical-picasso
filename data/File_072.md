## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This is the third annual DSUR for BRC-471 (Lumitanib), an investigational oral
dual EGFR/HER2 TKI under development for EGFR-mutant advanced NSCLC following
progression on osimertinib.

**Studies ongoing during the reporting period:**
- BIORCE-ONC-2023-001 (Phase II, n=120 target, n=89 enrolled at data cut)

**Key safety findings during this reporting period:**

The most significant safety finding was the ILD/pneumonitis signal at higher-than-
anticipated incidence. Four confirmed cases (6.0% incidence as of the October 2023
DSMB review) prompted Protocol Amendment 2 (November 2023) mandating baseline HRCT,
enhanced respiratory monitoring, and additional exclusion criteria. All four cases
resolved with drug hold and corticosteroids. No Grade 4 or fatal ILD events.

A DDI study confirmed 2.3-fold AUC increase with itraconazole (strong CYP3A4
inhibitor); updated guidance added to IB and SmPC draft.

**Overall benefit-risk:** Remains favourable. Phase II interim data (ORR 29%,
median PFS 5.4 months) demonstrate meaningful antitumour activity in a setting
with no approved standard of care. ILD signal consistent with EGFR inhibitor class;
manageable with enhanced monitoring.

## 6. SUMMARY OF SAFETY FINDINGS

### 6.1 ILD/Pneumonitis Signal

Cumulative incidence across both studies: 4/131 (3.1% all grades; 2/131 = 1.5%
Grade 3). Within Phase II study: 4/89 (4.5% all grades; 2/89 = 2.2% Grade 3).

All events occurred within the first 4 months of treatment. All responded to
drug hold and corticosteroids. No Grade 4 or fatal ILD observed.

The incidence exceeds the ~2% rate for osimertinib (FLAURA) but is within the
range for dual EGFR/HER2 inhibitors (2-8%). Considered a known class effect,
manageable with appropriate monitoring.

### 6.2 CYP3A4 Drug-Drug Interaction

DDI study (n=12 healthy volunteers): 2.3-fold increase in BRC-471 AUC with
itraconazole. Incorporated into IB, SmPC draft, and protocol. Patients on
strong CYP3A4 inhibitors required to reduce dose to 100 mg BID or discontinue
the inhibitor.

### 6.3 Other Findings

- No new cardiac signals (mean QTcF change +3.1 ms; no QTcF >500 ms)
- No new hepatic signals beyond single Grade 3 ALT case
- No haematological toxicity of concern
- No cumulative or unexpected toxicity patterns identified

## 7. EVALUATION OF THE RISK-BENEFIT BALANCE

### 7.1 Benefit

BRC-471 demonstrates clinically meaningful antitumour activity in EGFR-mutant
NSCLC post-osimertinib — a setting with no approved standard of care and
historical ORRs of 8-12% with chemotherapy. Phase II interim ORR 29% (95% CI
17.6-42.9) substantially exceeds this benchmark. HER2-amplified subgroup
(ORR 44%) suggests a biomarker-selected population with particularly robust benefit.

### 7.2 Risk

Primary identified risk: ILD/pneumonitis (4.5% Phase II incidence, all manageable).
Known EGFR inhibitor class effect with established risk mitigation strategies.
Other risks (diarrhoea, rash, nausea, QT prolongation, DDI) are well-characterised.

### 7.3 Conclusion

Benefit-risk balance remains favourable. ILD signal appropriately addressed
through protocol amendment and IB update. Development continues as planned.
