Study: BIORCE-CAR-2024-007 | Version 1.0 | Date: 01 March 2024
================================================================================

TITLE: A Phase III Study of BRC-883 (Cardanezumab) in Patients with
Familial High Cholesterol Who Cannot Take Statins

WHY ARE YOU BEING INVITED?
You have Heterozygous Familial Hypercholesterolaemia (HeFH) -- a genetic
condition causing high LDL cholesterol -- and cannot tolerate statins.

WHAT IS THE PURPOSE?
We are testing BRC-883 (Cardanezumab), a monthly injection that blocks a
protein called PCSK9 to lower LDL cholesterol. In earlier studies (n=148):
  - LDL-C reduced by average 57%
  - No heart attacks or strokes occurred
  - Injection site reactions (mild redness) in ~1 in 12 patients

WHAT WILL HAPPEN?
  - 1 in 2 chance of receiving BRC-883 (vs placebo)
  - Injection every 4 weeks at the clinic (not self-injected at home)
  - 52 weeks treatment + 24 weeks follow-up
  - Blood tests (fasting) at every visit

POSSIBLE RISKS
  - Injection site reaction (redness, mild pain): ~8% patients, mild
  - Liver enzyme rise: rare (1-2%); resolved on drug hold
  - Unknown risks possible -- closely monitored throughout

VOLUNTARY
Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time.
