Mechanisms of Resistance to Third-Generation EGFR Inhibitors in
Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer: A Systematic Review

DOI: 10.1200/PO.21.00500 (fictional DOI)

ABSTRACT

PURPOSE
  Osimertinib is the standard first-line treatment for EGFR-mutant advanced NSCLC.
  However, resistance is nearly universal. The molecular landscape of osimertinib
  resistance is heterogeneous. This systematic review synthesises current knowledge.

METHODS
  Systematic search: PubMed, EMBASE, Cochrane Library, January 2018 to December 2021.
  Studies reporting molecular profiling of osimertinib-resistant NSCLC included.
  47 studies encompassing 3,218 patients analysed.

RESULTS
  Resistance mechanisms identified in approximately 70% of profiled patients.

  On-target (EGFR-dependent) mechanisms ~30%:
    C797S mutation:         ~19%
    EGFR amplification:      ~7%
    Other EGFR alterations:  ~4%

  Off-target (bypass) mechanisms ~40%:
    MET amplification:       ~15%
    HER2 amplification:      ~12%
    KRAS mutation:            ~5%
    PIK3CA mutation:          ~4%
    BRAF V600E:               ~3%
    Other:                    ~1%

  Histologic transformation to SCLC: ~5%
  No mechanism identified: ~30%

  Co-occurrence of multiple mechanisms in a single patient: ~22% of those
  with identified mechanisms.

CONCLUSIONS
  Osimertinib resistance is molecularly diverse. HER2 and MET amplification
  represent the most actionable targets. Comprehensive molecular profiling
  at progression -- ideally combining tissue biopsy with plasma ctDNA -- is
  recommended to guide treatment selection.


INTRODUCTION

Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) is the most common form of lung cancer,
accounting for approximately 85% of all cases. Somatic activating mutations
in EGFR are present in approximately 15% of Western and 40-50% of Asian NSCLC
patients. The development of EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) has
transformed management.

Third-generation TKIs, specifically osimertinib, are now standard first-line
treatment (FLAURA, FLAURA2 trials). Despite efficacy, acquired resistance
develops in virtually all patients after a median of 18-22 months. Unlike
first-generation TKI resistance (dominated by T790M), osimertinib resistance
is molecularly diverse, complicating the development of standard post-
osimertinib treatment algorithms.


METHODS

Search strategy: PubMed, EMBASE, and Cochrane Library using terms:
  ("osimertinib" OR "AZD9291" OR "tagrisso") AND
  ("resistance" OR "acquired resistance") AND
  ("NSCLC" OR "non-small cell lung cancer")

Eligible studies: prospective or retrospective studies reporting molecular
profiling at progression on osimertinib.

Quality assessment: Newcastle-Ottawa Scale for cohort studies.


RESULTS

47 studies included (n=3,218 patients, publication years 2018-2021).

Resistance mechanism frequency (above). Co-occurrence observed in 22%.

Clinical implications by mechanism:
  C797S: renders osimertinib inactive; novel 4th-gen TKIs in development
  MET amplification: capmatinib, tepotinib, savolitinib; Phase II promising
  HER2 amplification: dual EGFR/HER2 inhibitors; newer agents in development
  KRAS: G12C inhibitors active (sotorasib, adagrasib); others unmet need
  SCLC transformation: platinum/etoposide +/- atezolizumab recommended

Profiling sensitivity: ctDNA ~55-70%; tissue ~75-85%; combined ~90%.


DISCUSSION

The molecular heterogeneity of osimertinib resistance has profound implications
for drug development. Several strategies under evaluation:
  1. Matched targeted therapy by resistance mechanism
  2. Combinations addressing co-occurring mechanisms
  3. Chemotherapy + immunotherapy for uncharacterized resistance
  4. Novel agents with activity across multiple resistance mechanisms

Re-biopsy at progression is now widely endorsed in clinical guidelines.


CONCLUSIONS

Osimertinib resistance is molecularly diverse. Comprehensive profiling at
progression is essential. HER2 and MET amplification are the most actionable
mechanisms. Co-occurrence is common. Phase II/III trials in stratified
post-osimertinib populations expected to produce practice-changing data.
