Plasma phosphorylated tau 181 as an early pharmacodynamic marker for tau aggregation inhibition: post-hoc analysis of the VELAGOSTAT-002 trial

Summary

In a post-hoc analysis of the Phase II BIORCE-NEU-2020-002 trial (n=94), we evaluated whether plasma p-tau 181 at Week 26 predicted tau PET response at Week 52 in early Alzheimer's disease patients treated with BRC-209 (velagostat). Among 49 BRC-209-treated patients with complete data, a p-tau 181 reduction of ≥25% at Week 26 was observed in 31/49 (63%). Among early responders, 87% showed significant tau PET SUVR reduction at Week 52 (mean −41%), compared with 28% of non-responders (mean −18%; p<0.001). AUC 0.84. These findings support plasma p-tau 181 as an accessible early pharmacodynamic biomarker for BRC-209 target engagement.

Discussion

Our findings suggest early plasma p-tau 181 response during BRC-209 treatment is strongly predictive of subsequent tau PET benefit (AUC 0.84). If replicated prospectively, this could enable adaptive enrichment strategies in future trials. Limitations include the post-hoc, exploratory nature and small sample size. Prospective validation is planned in the Phase III BIORCE-NEU-2022-004 trial.
