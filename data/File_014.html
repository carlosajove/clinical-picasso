INFORMED CONSENT FORM

A Phase II Study of BRC-471 (Lumitanib) in Advanced Lung Cancer

IMPORTANT: Please read this document carefully. You may take it home. Ask any questions before signing. Participation is completely voluntary.

1. Introduction

You are being asked to take part in a clinical research study testing BRC-471 (Lumitanib), an experimental medicine, in patients with EGFR-mutant NSCLC that has progressed on osimertinib.

2. Purpose of This Study

We want to find out whether BRC-471 can shrink or stop your cancer, how long any benefit lasts, what side effects it causes, and how your body processes it.

3. Duration

You will take BRC-471 until your cancer grows or side effects are unmanageable. Most patients remain on treatment for several months to over a year. Follow-up visits every 12 weeks for at least one year after stopping.

4. What Will Happen

VisitWhat happens
Screening (up to 4 weeks)Blood tests, CT scan, ECG, physical exam, tumour biopsy if needed
Treatment cycles (28 days each)BRC-471 tablets twice daily; blood tests; CT every 8 weeks
End of TreatmentFull assessment — scans, blood tests, questionnaires
Follow-upVisits or calls every 12 weeks

You will take 2 tablets of BRC-471 twice a day. Also: ondansetron (anti-nausea) during the first week of each cycle. Do not crush or chew tablets.

6. Possible Benefits

You may benefit if BRC-471 shrinks or slows your cancer. This cannot be guaranteed.

7. Your Right to Withdraw

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty or effect on your standard medical care.

8. Privacy

Data processed per EU GDPR. Study documents use a participant code, not your name.

9. Costs

No cost to you. Medicine provided free. Reasonable travel expenses reimbursed.
