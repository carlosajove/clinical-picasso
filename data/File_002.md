## UPDATED EFFICACY DATA (Phase II 18-Month Extension)

Among 71 patients who completed the 18-month extension, BRC-209 produced:
- CDR-SB at 18 months: -0.54 points vs. placebo (p=0.035) — significant
- Tau PET SUVR (temporal composite): -28% vs. -4% placebo (p<0.001)
- Plasma p-tau 181: -34% vs. -1% (p=0.001) — novel finding
- Plasma NfL: No significant difference (p=0.31) — exploratory

The p-tau 181 reduction suggests target engagement extends to downstream
tau phosphorylation pathways, not just aggregation.

## CYP2C19 POOR METABOLIZERS (NEW)

A pharmacogenomic sub-study (n=18) identified 4 CYP2C19 poor metabolizers
with 2.8-fold higher BRC-209 AUC. Two experienced Grade 2 dizziness.
CYP2C19 genotyping recommended prior to enrollment; PM patients start at
200 mg QD (reduced dose).

## FALLS RISK GUIDANCE (UPDATED)

Falls incidence over 18 months: 19% (BRC-209) vs. 24% (placebo).
No excess risk attributable to BRC-209. Morse Fall Scale assessment
encouraged at each visit.
