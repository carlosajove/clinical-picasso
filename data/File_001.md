# CSR-005 — CONFERENCE ABSTRACT
## Submitted to: European Society for Medical Oncology (ESMO) Annual Congress 2023
## Submission Date: 14 April 2023 | Status: ACCEPTED (Oral Presentation)

**Title:** BRC-471 (Lumitanib), a Dual EGFR/HER2 Inhibitor, Demonstrates Encouraging
Activity in EGFR-Mutant NSCLC After Osimertinib: Phase II Interim Results

**Authors:** Vázquez E¹, Marchetti F², Kowalski A³, Dupont C⁴, Schmidt H⁵,
Tanaka Y⁶, Okonkwo B⁷, Chen L⁸, Fernández M¹
(1=Biorce Therapeutics; 2=Institut Curie; 3=Maria Sklodowska-Curie NRI;
4=Gustave Roussy; 5=Charité Berlin; 6=NCC Tokyo; 7=UCLH; 8=Shanghai Chest Hospital)

**Background:** Virtually all patients with EGFR-mutant NSCLC develop resistance
to osimertinib. BRC-471 is an oral dual EGFR/HER2 inhibitor designed to overcome
HER2-amplification and C797S-mediated resistance.

**Methods:** BIORCE-ONC-2023-001 is a Phase II, open-label, single-arm study.
Eligible patients had EGFR-mutant advanced NSCLC with documented progression on
osimertinib. All received BRC-471 200 mg BID continuously. Primary endpoint:
ORR per RECIST v1.1 (BICR). This interim report includes 55 evaluable patients
(data cut: 01 Sep 2023).

**Results:** Confirmed ORR: 29.1% (16/55; 95% CI 17.6-42.9). DCR: 70.9%.
Median PFS: 5.4 months (95% CI 4.1-7.2). Median DoR: 7.8 months (95% CI 5.1-NR).
HER2-amplified subgroup (n=18): ORR 44.4% (8/18). Grade >=3 TEAEs: 38%.
ILD/pneumonitis: 6% (all grades); 2 Grade 3 cases, both managed with drug
hold and steroids.

**Conclusions:** BRC-471 demonstrates clinically meaningful antitumour activity
in EGFR-mutant NSCLC post-osimertinib. Activity particularly pronounced in
HER2-amplified tumours. Enrolment ongoing; final data expected 2025.
