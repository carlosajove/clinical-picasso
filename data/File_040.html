What is this study about?

Why is this research being done?
We are testing BRC-209 (Velagostat) to see whether it can slow memory and thinking problems caused by early Alzheimer's disease. It works by trying to stop a harmful protein called tau from building up in brain cells.
Do I have to take part?
No. Participation is entirely your choice. If you decide not to join, or change your mind later, your regular medical care will not be affected in any way.

What will happen?

Which treatment will I receive?
You will be randomly allocated to receive either BRC-209 (the study medicine) or a placebo (a dummy tablet). You have a 2-in-3 chance of receiving BRC-209. Neither you, your carer, nor your doctors will know which you are taking. One tablet every morning for up to 96 weeks.

Possible Risks

BRC-209 is experimental. There may be unknown side effects. You will be carefully monitored.
