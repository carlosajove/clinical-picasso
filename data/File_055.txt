BIORCE THERAPEUTICS INITIATES PHASE III CLINICAL TRIAL OF BRC-209
(VELAGOSTAT) IN EARLY ALZHEIMER'S DISEASE

-- Phase III VELAD-3 Study Enrolling Patients Across Five Countries --
-- Builds on Significant Phase II Tau PET and Cognitive Results --

MADRID, SPAIN -- Biorce Therapeutics S.L., a clinical-stage biopharmaceutical
company focused on transformative therapies for neurological and metabolic
diseases, today announced the initiation of patient enrolment in the Phase III
VELAD-3 study (BIORCE-NEU-2022-004) evaluating BRC-209 (velagostat) in
patients with early Alzheimer's disease (AD).

"The initiation of the VELAD-3 Phase III study represents a pivotal milestone
for Biorce and for the Alzheimer's field," said Dr. Rafael Moreno, CEO of
Biorce Therapeutics. "Our Phase II data demonstrated statistically significant
target engagement -- a 31% reduction in tau PET signal -- along with encouraging
cognitive outcomes. Velagostat's mechanism, which targets tau aggregation rather
than amyloid clearance, offers a differentiated therapeutic strategy."

ABOUT THE VELAD-3 PHASE III STUDY

Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter Phase III
Patients: 480 (320 velagostat / 160 placebo), aged 50-80
Population: MCI or mild AD dementia, amyloid-confirmed, CDR global 0.5 or 1.0
Primary endpoint: Change from baseline in CDR-Sum of Boxes at Week 78
Treatment duration: 96 weeks
Sites: 42 sites across Spain, UK, Sweden, Netherlands, Canada
Registration: EudraCT 2022-003891-28; NCT05482910
First patient enrolled: 12 July 2022

PHASE II RESULTS

VELAD-2 study (BIORCE-NEU-2020-002, n=94, 18 months):
  Tau PET SUVR (entorhinal): -31% velagostat vs -4% placebo (p=0.003)
  CDR-SB slowing at 18 months: -0.54 points (p=0.035) -- 30% slowing
  Plasma p-tau 181: -34% vs -1% placebo (p=0.001)
  Safety: no ARIA, no hepatotoxicity, no serious CNS adverse events

ABOUT BRC-209 (VELAGOSTAT)

Velagostat is an oral tau aggregation inhibitor. It stabilises tau monomers
in a non-pathological conformation, preventing seeding and propagation of
tau aggregates. Unlike anti-amyloid therapies, it does not deplete amyloid
plaques, positioning it as potentially complementary to amyloid-targeting agents.
