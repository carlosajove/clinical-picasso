## 1. TITLE

A Phase II, Open-Label, Multicenter Study to Evaluate the Efficacy and
Safety of BRC-471 (Lumitanib) in Patients with EGFR-Mutant Advanced
Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer Following Progression on First-Line Osimertinib

## 2. PROTOCOL SYNOPSIS

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| Study Phase | Phase II |
| Design | Open-label, single-arm, multicenter |
| Primary Endpoint | ORR per RECIST v1.1 (BICR) |
| Secondary Endpoints | PFS, OS, DoR, DCR, safety |
| Target Enrollment | 120 patients |
| Duration | 36 months (12 enrolment + 24 follow-up) |
| Countries | Spain, France, Germany, Italy |

## 3. BACKGROUND

NSCLC accounts for ~85% of lung cancer cases. EGFR-activating mutations
present in ~15% of Western patients. Osimertinib is now standard first-line
treatment but resistance is universal after a median of 18–22 months.
Resistance mechanisms include C797S mutation (~19%), MET amplification (~15%),
HER2 amplification (~12%). BRC-471 is a novel dual EGFR/HER2 inhibitor
designed to overcome HER2-amplification and C797S-mediated resistance.

## 4. STUDY OBJECTIVES

**Primary:** Evaluate antitumour activity — ORR per RECIST v1.1 by BICR.

**Secondary:** PFS, OS, DoR, DCR, safety & tolerability, PK parameters.

**Exploratory:** Biomarkers of response via ctDNA; PROs (EORTC QLQ-C30, LC13).

## 5. PATIENT ELIGIBILITY

**Key Inclusion:**
1. Age >=18 years
2. Confirmed advanced NSCLC (Stage IIIB/IV)
3. Documented activating EGFR mutation (ex19del or L858R)
4. Radiographic progression on/after first-line osimertinib
5. >=1 measurable lesion per RECIST v1.1
6. ECOG PS 0–2

**Key Exclusion:**
1. Prior EGFR/HER2 dual inhibitor
2. Active CNS metastases requiring steroids
3. History of ILD or drug-induced pneumonitis
4. QTcF >480 ms
5. Pregnancy or breastfeeding

## 6. INVESTIGATIONAL PRODUCT

BRC-471 200 mg BID orally (two 100 mg tablets), continuous 28-day cycles.
Dose reductions: 150 mg BID then 100 mg BID. Discontinue if below 100 mg BID.

## 7. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS

Simon two-stage design. H0: ORR=10%; H1: ORR=25%. Stage 1: 35 evaluable
patients — need >=8 responses to proceed. Stage 2: 110 evaluable. Power 80%,
one-sided alpha 0.10. Total 120 patients (10% non-evaluable assumed).
